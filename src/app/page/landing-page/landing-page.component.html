The smart nutrition chart for kids shows the recommended daily amounts of nutrients like protein, carbs, fats, vitamins, and minerals that support children's growth. It provides food sources for each nutrient, helping parents plan a varied, balanced, healthy diet.

Fats:

These are a source of energy and help the body absorb some vitamins. They also provide essential fatty acids that the body cannot make. The food sources mentioned are butter, oil, nuts, and seeds.

Iron:

This is a mineral that helps the blood carry oxygen to the cells. It also supports the immune system and brain function. The food sources listed are meat, fish, spinach, and beans.

Carbohydrates:

These are the body’s main source of energy. The food sources mentioned are bread, rice, pasta, and cereal.

Protein:

This is essential for growth and repair in the body. The food sources listed are meat, fish, eggs, beans, and nuts.

Calcium:

This is necessary for strong bones and teeth. The food sources mentioned are milk, cheese, and yogurt.

Vitamin C:

This is important for growth, development, and repair of all body tissues. The food source listed is oranges.

List of 10 commandments for healthy eating in Tagalog, referred to as “10 Kumainments”

1. Kumain ng iba’t ibang pagkain:

Eat a variety of foods every day to get the nutrients needed by the body.

Sa unang anim na buwan ni baby, breastfeeding lamang at mula six months, bigyan din siya ng ibang angkop na pagkain:

Breastfeed infants exclusively from birth up to six months then give appropriate complementary foods while continuing breastfeeding for two years and beyond for optimum growth and development.

Kumain ng gulay at prutas araw-araw:

Eat more vegetables and fruits everyday to get the essential vitamins, minerals, and fiber for regulation of body processes.

Kumain ng isda karne, at iba pang pagkaing may protina:

Consume fish, lean meat, poultry, egg, dried beans, or nuts daily for growth and repair of body tissues.

Uminom ng gatas at kumain ng pagkaing mayaman sa calcium:

Consume milk, milk products, and other calcium-rich foods such as small fish and shellfish everyday for healthy bones and teeth.

Tiyaking malinis at ligtas ang ating pagkain at tubig:

Consume safe foods and water to prevent diarrhea and other food and water-borne diseases.

Gumamit ng iodized salt:

Use iodized salt to prevent Iodine Deficiency Disorders.

Hinay-hinay sa maaalat, mamantika, at matatamis:

Limit intake of salty, fried, fatty, and sugar-rich foods to prevent cardiovascular diseases.

Panatilihin ang tamang timbang:

Attain normal body weight through proper diet and moderate physical activity to maintain good health and help prevent obesity.

Maging aktibo. Iwasan ang alak at huwag manigarilyo:

Be physically active, make healthy food choices, manage stress, avoid alcoholic beverages, and do not smoke to help prevent lifestyle-related non-communicable diseases.

The Importance of non-nutrients in a child’s diet.

Antioxidants:

These are molecules that protect our body from harmful toxins and free radicals. They are found in bright-colored vegetables and fruits like melons, berries, carrots, and red cabbage. Antioxidants are known for their anti-inflammatory abilities. They may protect cells in your body from free radical damage that can occur from exposure to certain chemicals, smoking, pollution, radiation, and as a byproduct of normal metabolism. Antioxidants are found in many foods and include selenium, vitamin A and related carotenoids, vitamins C and E, plus various phytochemicals such as lycopene, lutein, and quercetin.

Dietary Fiber:

This is a form of carbohydrate that helps in reducing bloating. Dietary fiber increases the weight and size of your stool and softens it, making it easier to pass and decreasing your chance of constipation. It also helps maintain bowel health, lowers cholesterol levels, helps control blood sugar levels, aids in achieving a healthy weight, and helps you live longer. Dietary fiber is found mainly in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and legumes.

Phytonutrients:

Phytonutrients are natural chemicals or compounds produced by plants. They keep plants healthy, protecting them from insects and the sun. Phytonutrients also have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that can help support a healthy human body. There are thousands of phytonutrients found in plants and related foods. Some of the most common phytonutrients are carotenoids, ellagic acid, resveratrol, flavonoids, phytoestrogens, and glucosinolates5. Phytonutrients play an essential role in maintaining health and preventing disease. They fight oxidative stress, reduce inflammation, and enhance immunity.

Sign and Treatment of Malnutrition

Signs of Malnutrition:

Parents should watch for signs like a lack of appetite in children whose weight and height haven’t been assessed by a healthcare provider.

Appetite Test:

Healthcare providers will conduct an appetite test using RUTF (Ready-to-Use Therapeutic Food), observing the number of mouthfuls a child takes within an hour.

Treatment Options:

hildren with a good appetite receive RUTF as outpatient treatment, while those with no appetite require in-patient care.

Additional Sickness:

If a child has a lack of appetite plus any sickness such as diarrhea, they should be treated at a health facility specializing in Community-based Management of Acute Malnutrition.

The Faces Of Malnutrition

Underweight Children:

These children have low weight for their age. This form of malnutrition is the easiest to address, often by increasing food intake.

Stunted Children:

These children are short for their age due to chronic under-nutrition. The effects become irreversible by age 5 and affect 3 million Filipino children under 5 years old.

Wasted Children:

They have low weight for height, indicating acute malnutrition. This affects 700,000 Filipino children under five years old. Those with severe acute malnutrition are nine times more likely to die than well-nourished children.
